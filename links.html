Links

Princeton University Society of Fellows

I am currently Link-Cotsen Fellow at the Society of Fellows in the Liberal Arts, Princeton University.

Princeton University Sociology Department

My department at Princeton University, where I am spending time with the Center for the Study of Social Organization led by Paul DiMaggio and Viviana Zelizer, and teaching courses in Sociology of Science, Sociology of Technology, and Human-Computer Interaction.

Center for Information Technology Policy

I am Associated Faculty with the Center for Information Technology Policy at Princeton, run by Ed Felten. The Center's fabulous visitors and speaker lineups keep the Princeton community thinking hard about the role of information technology in society.

Digital Humanities at Princeton

I'm on the executive committee of the Digital Humanities Working Group at Princeton University, where I've been coordinating efforts to produce a campus-wide Digital Humanities Initiative. This is bringing together not only scholars from across the humanities and computational sciences, but also staff members from Princeton's museums, libraries, and Office of Information Technology.

The SpaceTeams Collaboration

My resesarch group at Princeton and UCI, funded by an NSF grant in Socio-Computational Systems.

The Mars Exploration Rover Mission

My dissertation fieldsite, featuring two amazing robots on Mars and a host of extraordinary people on Earth who work with them every day.

Cassini Mission to Saturn

The NASA-ESA-ASI Cassini Mission to Saturn, my postdoctoral research site and where the action is in the outer solar system.

Informatics and Computer Sciences at University of California, Irvine

The department where I worked as a postdoctoral researcher from 2009-2010 with Professor Paul Dourish's research group, and with the LUCI Laboratory for Ubiquitous Computing and Interaction

Department of Science and Technology Studies, Cornell University

My PhD department, where I learned how to study science and technology in social context.

Culturally Embedded Computing

My research group at Cornell University, led by Phoebe Sengers, challenging our understanding of computing in social contexts.

Department of History and Philosophy of Science

My department at Cambridge University, where I studied seventeenth and eighteenth century history of representation in science, and produced a display case for the Whipple Museum.
